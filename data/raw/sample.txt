Artificial Intelligence (AI) is transforming industries across the globe. From healthcare to finance, AI systems are being used to automate processes, analyze large datasets, and improve decision-making.

Generative AI is a subset of AI that focuses on creating new content such as text, images, audio, and code. Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-style models are trained on massive datasets and can generate human-like responses.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) improves the reliability of generative models by combining information retrieval with text generation. Instead of relying only on pre-trained knowledge, the system retrieves relevant documents and uses them to generate grounded responses.

As AI systems become more advanced, designing scalable and modular architectures becomes essential. Proper separation of ingestion, retrieval, and generation layers ensures maintainability and performance in production systems.
